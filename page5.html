SIRENS

I woke up to the sounds of the ambulance
wailing in my ears,
the weep of dolor
resonating in my tears.
My serene world of innocence
was left with the sound of sirens,
The sail of the adolescent,
smooth and young,
was tainted by the hurricane of detriment.
It rang like a wild beast,
like the anguish at a feast.
crippling slowly
when you expect it least,
whispers of baby's breath
that's calming and warm
until it blocks your hearing,
posing harm.
I knew it, like the first time that it happened,I knew it.
They're back once again.
Those who pretended to care but will leave you in the end.
I choose to believe
that life is the bitter truth
in the world's lie.
I choose to believe that everything will be alright,
but will it be?
I know that it will.
So i hold on to that very thin string of hope,
that very thin light that I see,
as i fall into the abyss of despair.
I'd rather wake up to the sounds of the ambulance,
because I know that the sounds that I will wake up to will ignite the dying fire of hope in my heart.
I'd rather wake up to the call of dear life,
I'd rathe feel the warmth of the sun,
and hear the ambulance
than hear the cry of the world
who lost one truth in the sea of lies.
I choose to believe because I know that it will be alright,
that the sounds of the ambulance will once go away,
not because my hope is over,
but because I wouldn't need the ambulance anymore.
-Cholo Perez
